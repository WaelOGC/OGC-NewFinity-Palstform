## 1. Introduction

This document defines the procedures, standards, and governance rules for community voting within the OGC NewFinity ecosystem.

It establishes how users participate in decision-making, how proposals are evaluated, and how outcomes are enforced.

The voting system ensures:

- Transparent decision-making
- Fair participation
- Democratic input from users
- Accountability and traceability
- Scalable governance aligned with future DAO transitions

This is the official v1.0 off-chain voting framework.

## 2. Voting Principles

### 2.1 Transparency

All votable proposals must be publicly documented, with results visible to the entire community.

### 2.2 Fair Access

All eligible community members may vote under equal rules.

### 2.3 Accountability

Voting outcomes must be respected and implemented according to documented procedures.

### 2.4 Security

Voting systems must prevent abuse, manipulation, or double voting.

### 2.5 Gradual Decentralization

Voting rules will evolve toward on-chain governance as the ecosystem matures.

## 3. Votable Proposal Types

### 3.1 Platform Feature Proposals

Decisions related to:

- New features
- Feature upgrades
- UI/UX enhancements
- Content expansions

### 3.2 Challenge System Proposals

Including:

- New challenge categories
- Seasonal events
- Scoring method adjustments
- Reward distribution updates

### 3.3 Policy Proposals

Changes to:

- Community Standards
- Content guidelines
- Participation rules

### 3.4 Economic & Token Proposals (Future)

Including:

- Reward pool adjustments
- Token sink mechanisms
- Staking & governance rules

## 4. Eligibility Requirements

### 4.1 Eligible Voters

Users must meet at least one of the following:

- Verified platform account
- Completed minimum onboarding
- Participated in at least one challenge
- Held OGC tokens (future)

### 4.2 Ineligible Participants

Users are excluded if:

- Under suspension
- Flagged for abuse or fraud
- Using duplicate or fake accounts

## 5. Proposal Workflow

### 5.1 Proposal Lifecycle

- Draft submission
- Internal screening
- Public posting for review
- Community discussion period
- Voting period
- Vote tallying
- Decision publication
- Implementation phase

### 5.2 Required Proposal Format

- Title
- Summary
- Category
- Motivation
- Proposed change
- Expected impact
- Risks & considerations
- Implementation plan
- Timeline

## 6. Voting Methods

### 6.1 Binary Voting

Options:

- Yes
- No

Used for:

- Simple policy updates
- UI updates
- Minor platform changes

### 6.2 Ranked Voting (Future)

Used when selecting among multiple options.

### 6.3 Weighted Voting (Future)

Voting weight based on:

- Token holdings
- Reputation score
- Contribution history

### 6.4 Quadratic Voting (Future)

Designed to reduce influence concentration.

## 7. Voting Periods

### 7.1 Standard Voting Window

- 72 hours
- Suitable for most proposals

### 7.2 Extended Voting Window

- 7 days
- Used for major system changes

### 7.3 Emergency Vote

- 24 hours
- For urgent decisions requiring rapid action

## 8. Quorum & Approval Requirements

### 8.1 Minimum Participation Threshold

A proposal is valid only if at least:

- 10% of eligible voters participate (initial rule)

### 8.2 Approval Rules

Binary votes require:

- Simple Majority ( > 50% )

Ranked votes (future):

- Option with highest weighted ranking wins

Weighted or staked voting (future):

- Follows governance council-approved rules

## 9. Vote Integrity & Security

### 9.1 Anti-Abuse Controls

- Duplicate vote detection
- Account validation checks
- IP anomaly detection
- Fraud pattern detection
- Anti-bot mechanisms

### 9.2 Transparency Requirements

- Public vote counts
- Public final tallies
- Clear record of decision

### 9.3 Privacy Requirements

- Anonymous voting allowed
- No personal data published
- Voting logs must be hashed

## 10. Implementation of Voting Outcomes

### 10.1 Binding Decisions

If a proposal passes:

- It must be implemented within the published timeline
- Responsible teams receive implementation tasks

### 10.2 Rejected Proposals

May be:

- Revised and resubmitted
- Shelved permanently
- Deferred to future governance phases

### 10.3 Post-Decision Review

Includes:

- Impact evaluation
- Feedback gathering
- Report published to community

## 11. Emergency Governance Procedures

### 11.1 Emergency Vote Trigger

Used for:

- Critical security issues
- Platform-wide outages
- Abuse detection incidents
- Token-related emergencies (future)

### 11.2 Emergency Protocol

- Immediate proposal drafting
- 24-hour voting window
- Fast execution (under 72 hours)

## 12. Recordkeeping & Archiving

### 12.1 Proposal Storage

Files stored under:

`/governance/proposals/{id}.md`

### 12.2 Voting Records

Stored under:

`/governance/votes/{proposal-id}.json`

### 12.3 Permanent Accessibility

All proposal histories remain available to users.

## 13. Future Enhancements

- Full on-chain voting
- Real-time voting dashboards
- Mobile voting integration
- Reputation-based voting systems
- DAO-level autonomous decision loops

## 14. Conclusion

This Community Voting Procedures & Decision Policy establishes a transparent, structured, and fair decision-making framework for the OGC NewFinity ecosystem, supporting both current off-chain governance and future decentralized governance layers.
