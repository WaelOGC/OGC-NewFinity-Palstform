# OGC NewFinity — Ecosystem Compliance & Risk Assessment Guidelines (v1.0)

## 1. Introduction

This document defines the compliance standards, regulatory obligations, risk assessment procedures, and ecosystem-wide safety policies for OGC NewFinity.

The guidelines ensure:

- Legal compliance across jurisdictions
- Safe user participation
- Reduced platform and token risk
- Consistent risk evaluation
- Transparent reporting
- Alignment with future decentralized governance

These rules apply to platform operations, user activities, content, token utility, governance, and ecosystem integrations.

## 2. Compliance Objectives

### 2.1 Core Goals

- Protect users
- Reduce organizational liability
- Maintain platform integrity
- Ensure global legal compliance
- Support safe token ecosystem development

### 2.2 Regulatory Framework Considerations

Includes:

- GDPR
- COPPA (if youth users involved)
- ePrivacy
- Global digital asset guidelines
- Anti-fraud and anti-abuse policies
- Intellectual property laws

## 3. Compliance Domains

### 3.1 Data Protection & Privacy

Covers:

- Data minimization
- Secure data storage
- User consent management
- User rights (access, deletion, export)

### 3.2 Content Compliance

Ensures:

- No harmful or illegal content
- No personal data exposure
- No defamation or discrimination
- Safe content for youth participants

### 3.3 Financial & Token Compliance

Includes:

- Non-security classification
- Token disclaimers
- Anti-speculation policies
- Local digital asset regulations
- Fraud and AML risk awareness

### 3.4 Operational Compliance

Includes:

- Vendor assessments
- Internal access rules
- Audit logs
- Secure deployment standards

## 4. Risk Assessment Framework

### 4.1 Risk Categories

- Operational Risk — system failures, outages
- Security Risk — unauthorized access, attacks
- Legal Risk — regulatory breaches
- Financial Risk — token misuse, reward exploitation
- Reputational Risk — community backlash
- User Safety Risk — harmful content, abuse

### 4.2 Risk Scoring Model

Risk is assessed by:

- Impact (1–5)
- Likelihood (1–5)
- Urgency (1–3)

Final formula:

**Risk Score = Impact × Likelihood × Urgency**

### 4.3 Risk Levels

- 1–10 → Low
- 11–30 → Medium
- 31–75 → High

## 5. Risk Mitigation Strategies

### 5.1 Technical Safeguards

- Encryption
- Access control
- Rate limiting
- Abuse detection
- Audit logs
- Data backup & recovery

### 5.2 Operational Safeguards

- Internal approvals
- Staff training
- Regular security audits
- Zero-trust access model

### 5.3 Governance Safeguards

- Reviewer oversight
- Community reporting
- Incident escalation protocols
- Security Council involvement

## 6. Compliance Requirements for Challenges

### 6.1 General Rules

Challenge content must:

- Follow editorial & safety guidelines
- Avoid dangerous or illegal activities
- Avoid biased or discriminatory tasks
- Include safe participation instructions

### 6.2 Youth Participation

If minors participate:

- Age restrictions must be clear
- No complex legal tasks
- Mandatory parental guidance notes
- No exposure to unsafe content

### 6.3 Submission Compliance

User submissions must be checked for:

- Plagiarism
- IP infringement
- Sensitive personal data
- Harmful content or threats

## 7. Token Ecosystem Compliance

### 7.1 Token Usage Rules

Token mechanisms must:

- Avoid securities-like behavior
- Maintain utility-only purpose
- Use clear disclaimers
- Prevent speculative framing

### 7.2 Fraud Prevention

Includes:

- Anti-sybil protections
- Duplicate account detection
- Suspicious transaction monitoring (future)

### 7.3 Token Distribution Oversight

Governance Council ensures:

- Fair distribution
- Sustainable reward models
- Risk-aligned adjustments

## 8. Vendor & Integration Compliance

### 8.1 Vendor Review Process

All partners must undergo:

- Legal compliance review
- Security capability assessment
- Data handling evaluation
- Contract and SLA validation

### 8.2 Integration Requirements

Third-party systems must:

- Support encryption
- Maintain GDPR compliance
- Provide audit logs
- Meet uptime and reliability standards

## 9. Incident Management & Reporting

### 9.1 Incident Categories

- Data breaches
- Security vulnerabilities
- Abuse reports
- Legal compliance issues
- Token manipulation attempts

### 9.2 Response Procedure

- Identify incident
- Verify severity level
- Notify Security Council
- Execute mitigation plan
- Publish post-incident report

### 9.3 Legal Reporting

Regulators must be informed when required by:

- GDPR
- Local digital asset regulations
- Law enforcement cooperation rules

## 10. Audits & Continuous Monitoring

- Compliance posture

### 10.2 External Audits (Future)

Includes:

- Security audits

- Regulatory compliance reviews

### 10.3 Continuous Monitoring

Track:

- Platform anomalies
- User behavior patterns
- Fraud indicators
- Token supply integrity
